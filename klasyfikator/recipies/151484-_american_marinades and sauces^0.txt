bearnaise sauce  in a small saucepan combine vinegar  wine  shallots  and dried  tarragon and simmer over moderate heat until reduced to  tablespoons  of liquid  cool and strain through a fine sieve     in the top of double boiler or a heat proof bowl whisk the egg yolks  until they become thick and sticky  whisk in the reduced vinegar  mixture and pepper  place the pan or bowl over a saucepan of  simmering  not boiling  water  whisk until mixture is warm  about   minutes   if mixture appears to become lumpy  dip pan immediately in  a bowl of ice water to cool  whisk until smooth and then continue  recipe   the yolk mixture has thickened enough when you can see the  bottom of the pan between strokes and mixture forms a light cream on  the wires of the whip     while whisking the yolk mixture gradually pour in the melted butter  a  tablespoon or so at a time whisking thoroughly to incorporate before  adding more butter  as the mixture begins to thicken and become  creamy  the butter can be added more rapidly  do not add the milk  solids at the bottom of the melted butter     season the sauce to taste with chopped tarragon  salt and pepper  to  keep the sauce warm  set the pan or bowl in lukewarm water or in a  thermos     yield   to    cups sauce        recipe by  cooking live  show  cl       sauce white wine vinegar  shallots minced salt and pepper  egg yolks  sticks unsalted butter   dried tarragon  dry white wine  minced fresh tarragon
